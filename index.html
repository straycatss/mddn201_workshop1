Manhole covers

These covers are access points to the underground networking in Wellington. These networks provide things like water (for mains, stormwater, and seweage), gas, telephone, eletricity, and internet to buildings around Wellington.

Security Cameras

Security cameras are used to watch over public and private area. The footage is then uploaoded most commonly to servers on the internet where it is stored so that it can be watched back if necessary.

Cabinets and Plinths

These above ground structures connect local internet networks to larger ones around them. They also connect individual homes to the wider network.

Powerlines

Powerlines provide the eletricity network which connects buildings with eletricity providers and producers.

Antenna

Antenna are used to recieve radio or televison waves. These signals are then translated into usable data by our devices. Antenna are used to pick up waves like AM and FM radio, wifi and 4g signals, Sky T.V. and more.

QR Codes

QR codes are a type of barcode which can be translated by a smartphone camera. Generally, they are used to take the scanner straight to a URL on the internet. Therefore, QR Codes physically connect people with the internet. They are used often on stickers or adversiting to take the viewer directly to a relevant website/piece of media. They were also used for fast contact tracing by the NZ government during the Covid-19 pandemic.
